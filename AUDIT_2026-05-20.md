## 0. Executive Summary

The hivemind is in better shape than the May 1 audit suggested. Most state-tracking files (`PROJECT_SNAPSHOT.md`, `phase-3-milestone-backlog.md`, `master-release-plan.md`, `strategic-context-map.md`, `Current_State.md`) are current as of 2026-05-20. The cross-agent notes file is correctly using its archive separator. The Coder and PM SKILL.md files are largely coherent. The benchmark system is healthy and operational.

**Three real problems remain, all addressable:**

1. **Dead `context/queue/` references** persist in 6 files three weeks after the May 1 audit flagged them. The directory was removed 2026-04-11; the references were never cleaned up. Every PM and Coder session encounters them in their startup protocol.
2. **Handoff files have grown unboundedly.** `coder-handoff.md` (70K) and `pm-handoff.md` (39K) contain inlined per-milestone closeouts going back to AMD-38 (2026-05-15). No agent reads either in full; the active state at the top is buried under historical milestone bodies. `coder-lessons.md` (56K) has the same growth pattern.
3. **No structured "Open Questions" surface.** The M3.6d-a session discovered seven source-vs-brief mismatches mid-coding (OR-M3-14). The PM has no dedicated artifact to consult *before* issuing the next coding instruction; OR-M3-NN entries live inside pm-handoff.md's "Open Risks" section, intermingled with closed risks and milestone summaries.

**Plus several smaller issues:**
- One stale skill-front-matter line ("M3.x will be downstream subsystems") in `project-manager/SKILL.md`.
- DEC ledger is **double-mirrored**, not triple (the prior brief overstated this). The mirror is intentional and self-documenting. Acceptable.
- The WUCP protocol document hardcodes one session-slug path (`/sessions/awesome-admiring-clarke/...`) in its `diff -rq` example. The PM's `freshness-preflight.md` correctly uses path-traversal logic; WUCP's example needs the same.
- Internal contradiction about `context/traceability/`: WUCP says traceability indexes live there; both CLAUDE.md files and the actual contents say only `TEMPLATE.md` lives there and real indexes are in `homesynapse-core/docs/traceability/`.
- The 5-week weekly-plan gap (W16–W20, Apr 13 – May 17) flagged in the May 1 audit is still present.
- Existing `AUDIT_REPORT.md` (2026-05-01, 32K) is itself stale — references M2.9 as latest, today is M3.6d-a.

The "FOUR OPEN" structural-ambiguity issue the project brief called out as the highest-profile anti-pattern has **already been corrected** in `coder-handoff.md`. The current text reads "ZERO OPEN — all resolved at commit. The prior 'FOUR OPEN' framing was inaccurate..." — fixed in the 2026-05-20 closeout.

### 6i. Additional anti-patterns I observed beyond the project brief's list

- **Append-only-without-eviction.** `coder-lessons.md` (56K), `strategic-lessons.md` (26K), and `cross-agent-notes.md` (18K) are all stated as append-only. Only `cross-agent-notes.md` has an explicit archive separator that's actually used. The other two grow forever. The brief's principle 8 ("Bounded growth") applies.

A frontmatter `state-type:` field would let agents triage faster.
- **`AUDIT_REPORT.md` at the top level is the wrong location.** Audits go in `context/audits/`; a top-level `AUDIT_REPORT.md` confuses orientation. Same observation applies to this file once the work lands — it should also move to `context/audits/`, leaving only README + setup.sh at the top level.
