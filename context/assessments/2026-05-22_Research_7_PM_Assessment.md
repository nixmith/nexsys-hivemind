## §1–§3 Strengths (kept for the record)

- **§Key Findings #3 cites HA WebSocket internals verbatim** — `homeassistant/components/websocket_api/http.py` constants (`MAX_PENDING_MSG=4096`, `PENDING_MSG_PEAK=2048`, `PENDING_MSG_PEAK_TIME=5`), PR #89611 reference, internal-comment rationale quote. This is the most rigorous primary-source citation in any research document so far.
- **§Key Findings #4 + REC-71 bcrypt analysis is exceptional.** JMH benchmark cited (patrickfav/bcrypt, `BcryptBenchmark.benchmarkBcBcryptLog 12 avgt 3 200,578 ± 25,269 ms/op`), Cortex-A72 extrapolation to 600–700ms, cost-10 + 5-minute SHA-256-keyed cache replacement design. The "two-tier hash-then-cache" pattern is genuinely the right answer for the home-server threat model and the analysis would survive external review.
- **§Key Findings #5 correctly identifies three-stage backpressure as the genuine differentiation.** Defended against all 7 surveyed peers — HA disconnects silently, Marten parks server-side, EventStoreDB persistent-subscriptions buffer-then-drop, SmartThings 429-rate-limits cloud. Per-entity LWW coalescing + global drop-oldest fallback is the right model. REC-72's codification at 256/1024/4096 frames + 2/5/30s lag is sound.
- **§Key Findings #7 + §5.4 INV-SE-02 disclosure** is the strongest application of the Research 5 v2 §5.4 lesson yet. ## Key Insights Internalized

1. **A perfect brief does not guarantee a clean §7.** The Research 7 brief was the most thoroughly constrained of any in the pipeline — verbatim `module-info.java` for 4 modules, verbatim `libs.versions.toml`, verbatim 38-type rest-api inventory, explicit NON-existent/upstream-constraint lists, LTD + INV register cross-references, and a §5.4 contradiction-disclosure mandate. The researcher still produced 15 §7 fabrications including a complete JPMS module-info rewrite. **Brief discipline is necessary but not sufficient.** The PM-side verification step (Steps E + F of the protocol) remains the load-bearing quality gate.

2. **The §7 fabrication pattern correlates with REC count + scope breadth, not brief quality.** Research 5 v2 had 9 RECs touching one module (configuration). Research 6 v1 had 12 RECs touching three modules. Research 7 v1 has 14 RECs touching four modules (rest-api, websocket-api, event-model, persistence) plus annotations and dependencies. Larger scope ↔ more surface ↔ more fabrication opportunities. **Recommendation:** for future research items targeting multiple modules, consider splitting into sub-research items per module (e.g., Research 7a = REST API, Research 7b = WebSocket API). This keeps the inventory verification surface manageable for the researcher.

3. **The Research 6 v1 JPMS module-name fabrication pattern recurred despite encoded counter-measures.** The brief's §"Cross-research-arc fabrication patterns to avoid" explicitly called out the Research 6 v1 JPMS-fabrication pattern and warned against it. The researcher ignored the warning. **The next research brief should escalate the verbatim-block visual prominence** — bold + boxed + repeated at the §7 directives. Possibly also: the brief should require the researcher to QUOTE the verbatim blocks back in §7 (so any fabrication produces a visible diff against the brief's own text).

4. **Duplicating-existing-types failure mode (REC-63 / F2) is a new pattern.** Prior research documents fabricated names for things that didn't exist; Research 7's REC-63 fabricated names for things that DO exist (`InvokeRequest` vs `CommandRequest`, `CommandReceipt` vs `CommandAcceptedResponse`). The researcher reads the existing types in the embedded inventory but doesn't notice they cover the proposed surface. **Mitigation for future briefs:** the "VERIFIED upstream constraints" section should call out specific endpoints that already exist (`POST /api/v1/entities/{entity_id}/commands` from Doc 09 §4.3) with the exact path/method/types — not just list the types in isolation.

5. **The PM's own verification depth matters more than the researcher's claimed grade.** Research 7's TL;DR claims a coherent design; the PM-side verification reveals 15 §7 issues. Without grep + cross-reference verification, the assessment would have approved fabricated module names and broken Java mechanics. **PM-side verification cost is non-negotiable** regardless of how clean the executive summary reads.

6. **The Research 7 substantive value is real and salvageable.** The platform survey is the most rigorous yet; the bcrypt analysis is genuinely valuable; the three-stage backpressure codification is sound; the INV-SE-02 disclosure pattern in §5.4 is the model for future research. The §7 fabrications do not negate the §§1-3 substance — they just mean the implementation work needs the PM-corrected disposition table above, not the researcher's raw §7.
