## §D2 note — the write-path-encryption boundary the crypto owner-doc MUST pin

D2 defers *operational* crypto-shredding to its first real consumer (post-MVP), and that is adopted. But "defer the operation" is only **regret-proof if the boundary is drawn at the irreversible seam, not the convenient one.** Crypto-shredding can only ever destroy data that was *written encrypted under a per-scope key in the first place*, and the event log is immutable — so the genuinely now-or-never decision is **from what point the sensitive event categories (presence/identity) are envelope-encrypted-on-write under per-scope DEKs.** The crypto owner-doc (M5-B / B1) must decide this *consciously*:

The crypto owner-doc must **pin two things, not one** (Nick, 2026-06-06): **(i) the at-rest posture for the event DB itself** (not just the secret store) — encrypted at rest at MVP, and *by what mechanism*: SQLCipher (whole-DB; LTD-03/dependency decision) vs **application-level per-scope payload encryption** (likely, with vanilla xerial sqlite-jdbc) — the latter being the *same machinery* that enables future per-category shred, so **INV-PD-03 (encrypt sensitive PII at rest) and INV-PD-07 (stay shreddable later) collapse into one decision**; and **(ii) from what point** sensitive categories are envelope-encrypted-on-write.

- **Decided posture (Nick — sharpens the earlier default):** two separable things are deferred and **only one is safe to defer.** **Defer the shred *operation* freely** (no MVP consumer). **But envelope-encrypt the sensitive PII categories (identity, person-linked presence) on write under per-scope DEKs from MVP** — because that is an **INV-PD-03 at-rest obligation in its own right** (plaintext PII on a Pi's removable storage is a live privacy hole) *and* keeps the launch-era corpus permanently shreddable with zero future migration; and since the per-scope key-management infrastructure is already MVP, it is a **small increment, not new scope.** The open variable is **Pi-4 write-path performance** on higher-frequency sensitive categories: resolve the exact category-by-category boundary with the **Lane D Pi-4 data** (the GC/GraalVM spikes carry an **AES-256-GCM write-path microbench**), falling a category back to whole-install-reset-only **only where Pi-4 perf genuinely forces it — consciously, not by omission.** (Costs M6 a little more than pure-defer; closes a permanent at-rest privacy gap — the maximally regret-proof line.) M5-D interviews carry the verifiable-erasure question as the re-scope-up trigger for the *operation*.
- **Amendment discipline:** keep the INV-PD-07 amendment **minimal** — strike only the "crypto-shredding must be operational for ≥1 category" clause; preserve the "MVP implements the per-scope key-management infrastructure + defines the encryption-scope categories" sentence and the design intent (`Architecture_Invariants_v1.md:425/:427`). **Widen it by one line** to also state the INV-PD-03 at-rest posture from (i), so both privacy invariants reconcile in one pass. **Full DOCS-Project review** — this narrows a constitutional privacy invariant the moat rests on; P4's lightweight block-track does **not** apply. **Assign the AMD number at authoring** (66–71 = M6 config; Research 7 reserved 72–85 — clean number per P2 assign-at-authoring).
