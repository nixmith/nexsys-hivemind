# Lane A2 Return — Transport + Hardware (commissioning, border-router reality, the DUT shortlist)

## §0 — Executive digest (≤1 page)

**The transport picture is two-sided, and both sides are load-bearing.** By raw native-SKU count the 2026 retail Matter market is WiFi-dominated — the best per-transport tracker shows **~753 native Matter devices: ~73% WiFi, ~29% Thread, ~4% Ethernet** (multi-radio overlap; Ethernet is almost entirely hubs) — but the split is **actuators-on-WiFi, sensing-on-Thread**: battery contact sensors are **100% Thread (zero WiFi options exist)**, battery motion/buttons/most locks likewise, and the entire 21-product IKEA budget line is Thread (§1). A WiFi/Ethernet-only V1 controller honestly covers the plug/bulb/appliance market plus everything behind Matter bridges, and is visibly locked out of the *event-driven sensing and security layer* — its native sensor story reduces to a handful of mains-powered mmWave presence units (the Meross MS600 class). The deferral consequence: Thread is where the *triggers* live, and new 2026 device families are Thread-leaning (§1.4).

**The banked OTBR fragility record stands and grew** (§2): ot-br-posix is healthy (monthly releases, v2026.07.0) but IPv6 RA/RIO routing is still the #1 failure class in 2026 reports; openHAB's "requires strong expertise in IPv6 routing" wording is unchanged this week. Thread 1.4 credential sharing remains uneven — Samsung shipped, Apple registered-but-no-UI, Google unreliable, Amazon still 1.3, self-hosted OTBR consumes datasets manually. RCP candidates are priced in §2.2; the cleanest official-firmware dedicated-radio story is the **HA Connect ZBT-2 ($48.95)**; the reference-cheapest is the **nRF52840 dongle ($11.02)**. Coexistence with the live ch20/0x774c fleet is solvable with physics, not hope: pin the future Thread network to **802.15.4 channel 25 (2475 MHz)** — 25 MHz from the Zigbee fleet, outside WiFi 11's occupancy — plus USB-2-ports-with-extension-cables discipline (the Intel USB3-interference record) and ~1 m antenna separation (§2.4). Nothing in this return touches the live fleet.

**Commissioning, rated headless** (§3): IP/on-network commissioning and multi-admin second-fabric join (ECM) are works-headless-today; BLE→WiFi and BLE→Thread work-with-caveats (live crash classes #164/#806; the Thread network-name gap closed 2026-07-13 — very fresh); SoftAP and NFC are gaps (SoftAP dead in the reference SDK; NFC spec-only since 1.6, zero shipped implementations). The BlueZ-over-D-Bus GPL fence is confirmed current. Every phase-1 WiFi DUT is factory-new-BLE-commissioned OR bootstrappable via a phone ecosystem + ECM — a real de-risk path for the bench.

**The DUT shortlist (§4, lift-ready for B3):** phase-1 core = **Tapo P110M** (the plug that actually exposes Matter 1.3 energy clusters — Kasa KP125M/Tapo P125M do NOT and are rejected), **Tapo L535E** bulb, **Meross MS600** mains presence sensor (the only clear native-WiFi Matter sensor class), **Aqara Hub M3 + one Zigbee child** as the deliberate bridge-provenance rig: **$265.94** core, $318.93 with the Meross MSS315 backup-energy-plug add. Phase-2 (priced, deferred) = **Eve Energy + Eve Door & Window + two IKEA Thread sensors + the RCP radio**: **$131.18** (ZBT-2 variant) or $93.25 (nRF52840 variant). **The Gen4 Shellys are restated stimulus-only in §4.0 — they appear in no DUT capacity**, and this lane recommends excluding ALL Shelly SKUs from wave 1 to keep the stimulus/DUT boundary unambiguous.

**Isolation (§5):** three concrete options, no winner — (A) bench SSID same-L2 ($0, zero blast-radius protection), (B) VLAN + mDNS reflection (~$90–260; the documented-flaky path: IPv6-mDNS reflection is broken in the standard tools, and it re-exposes the conntrack subscription-death hazard), (C) dedicated bench AP + dual-homed controller (~$35–110; the community-validated isolated shape — the controller sits ON the bench segment, link-local IPv6 suffices, zero RAs needed). **Custody (§6):** the full credential taxonomy is mapped — the fabric CA private key is the crown jewel; chip-tool keeps it Base64-unencrypted in `/tmp` by default (fabric lost on reboot); matter.js persists it in `~/.matter` contexts; cold-copy backup is documented-valid; nothing is portable between stacks.

### 1.4 Conclusion-to-evidence: what a WiFi/Ethernet-only V1 controller honestly covers

**Covers** (~70% of native SKUs by tracker count, skewed to actuators): near-total smart-plug coverage · roughly half the bulb market (Govee/Tapo/Meross/Third Reality/WiZ) · robot vacuums and large appliances (essentially 100% WiFi) · in-wall modules and wall dimmers · mains-powered mmWave presence (the Meross MS600 short-list — the ONLY native sensing open to it; §1.2) · a handful of WiFi locks · **everything behind a Matter bridge** (Hue Bridge, Aqara hubs, SwitchBot hubs, Dirigera all attach via WiFi/Ethernet), at the cost of per-vendor hub purchases and bridge-attested provenance (the A4 design row) [inference — assembled from §1.1–1.3 evidence].

**Visibly lacks (effectively closed, native, no bridge):** battery contact sensors (100% Thread) · battery motion/presence · buttons/remotes (no WiFi button located anywhere) · most locks (30/37) · water-leak/temp-humidity/air-quality battery sensors · the entire IKEA budget line · Eve's entire catalog (Thread-only vendor) [VERIFIED-current: matterdevices.io category pages, fetched 2026-07-19] [inference — category closure from the counts].

**The honest framing for B2/B3:** a WiFi/Ethernet-first V1 covers the *actuator and appliance* market and reaches sensors only via bridges (provenance-diluted) or one mains-presence class; **the devices that make automations trigger live disproportionately on Thread, and the market is moving further that way.** WiFi-first remains the right *sequencing* (no TBR ops burden, §2), but the return states plainly: it is a phase, not a resting place [inference — conclusion from §1.2–1.3].

## §2 — Thread border-router reality, updated ↻

### 2.1 OTBR-on-Pi: the fragility record stands (and grew)

- **Project health is not the problem:** ot-br-posix is on a monthly release cadence — latest **v2026.07.0 (2026-07-01)**; v2026.06.0 brought DNS-SD Browser/Resolver APIs, removal of the Avahi-publisher dependency, and REST-server hardening — **nothing specifically about RA/RIO behavior** [VERIFIED-current: https://github.com/openthread/ot-br-posix + /releases, fetched 2026-07-19]. The documented Pi path is unchanged (Raspberry Pi OS + nRF52840 reference RCP; "The quickest way to set up an OTBR is by using Docker") [VERIFIED-current: https://openthread.io/guides/border-router/prepare, fetched 2026-07-19].
- **IPv6 RA/RIO is still the #1 failure class** — 2026 exhibits: "Linux ignores Route Info Options by default when IPv6 forwarding is enabled" (required `accept_ra=2` + `accept_ra_rt_info_max_plen=64`; author called IPv6 routing the worst part of the build, May 2026) [community-reported: https://vort.org/blog/2026/05/15/building-thread-border-router-with-an-espressif-development-kit/] · misconfigured IPv6 is "the #1 silent failure mode" (openHAB dual-OTBR tutorial, Feb 2026) [community-reported: https://community.openhab.org/t/how-to-build-a-redundant-and-resilient-thread-network-for-matter-with-openthread/168220] · two BRs advertising identical OMR prefixes → ECMP duplicate-nexthop routes → "devices appearing offline in Home Assistant" (openthread discussion #12583, Feb 2026, resolution unconfirmed) [community-reported: https://github.com/orgs/openthread/discussions/12583] · OTBR add-on firewall/IPv6 teardown warnings + Thread devices unreachable externally (HA addons #4552, Apr 2026, open) [community-reported: https://github.com/home-assistant/addons/issues/4552] · Thread stuck detached on Pi 4 across OS versions (HA core #170452, May 2026, open) [community-reported: https://github.com/home-assistant/core/issues/170452]. The canonical historic root-cause writeup ("ghost" RIO routes — "it looked like they had 12 border routers… they only had 2") remains the best description of the domain [community-reported: https://github.com/home-assistant/operating-system/discussions/2333].
- **openHAB's wording is unchanged this week:** OTBR "requires strong expertise in IPv6 routing"; it still strongly recommends pairing via a commercial TBR first [VERIFIED-current: https://raw.githubusercontent.com/openhab/openhab-addons/main/bundles/org.openhab.binding.matter/README.md, fetched 2026-07-19]. The banked "workable-but-fragile" verdict [banked: roadmap return §3.3] is **not softened**; 2026 improvements are real but partial (BR CLI `multiail`/`br peers` diagnostics aimed exactly at multi-BR routing confusion [VERIFIED-current: https://github.com/openthread/openthread/blob/main/src/cli/README_BR.md, fetched 2026-07-19]; the mDNS path simplified; the firewall area still a live defect class).

### 2.4 Coexistence: physical-layer honesty vs the live ch20/0x774c fleet (UNTOUCHABLE — all adaptation is Thread-side)

- **Mapping:** 802.15.4 2.4 GHz: 16 channels, 2 MHz wide, 5 MHz spacing, f = 2405 + 5×(ch−11) MHz → **ch 15 = 2425, ch 20 = 2450 (the live fleet), ch 25 = 2475, ch 26 = 2480** [VERIFIED-current: https://www.nxp.com/docs/en/application-note/JN-AN-1079.pdf + Silabs AN1017 (hy-line mirror), fetched 2026-07-19]. WiFi 1/6/11 occupy ~2401–2423 / 2426–2448 / 2451–2473 (~22 MHz each) — the classic gap channels 15/20/25/26 are confirmed at source ("channels 15, 20, 25, and 26 fall between or outside the standard WiFi allocations") [VERIFIED-current: NXP JN-AN-1079].
- **Thread channel for the NEW network — pin it at dataset creation:** **primary ch 25 (2475)** — 25 MHz from the Zigbee fleet, outside WiFi 11's occupancy (2 MHz above its edge; inside the sideband lobe, so keep the Thread radio physically away from any WiFi AP [VERIFIED-current: https://www.oscium.com/training/zigbee-wifi-coexistence — "channel 25 is caught in Wi-Fi channel 11's sideband lobe"]); **alternate ch 26 (2480)** — max distance (30 MHz; meets NXP's ≥7 MHz interferer-offset guidance) but band-edge TX-power/device-support caveats [community-reported: Oscium; derating specifics = gap §8]; **rejected ch 15** — only ~1–2 MHz margin to WiFi 1 and 6 sidebands [inference — from the occupancy table]. Pinning: `dataset init new` → `dataset channel 25` → `dataset commit active` [VERIFIED-current: https://github.com/openthread/openthread/blob/main/src/cli/README_DATASET.md, fetched 2026-07-19]. Assume the dataset (channel included) propagates to any future BR via credential sharing — pick it right at formation [inference].
- **USB physical-layer discipline (applies to the bench regardless of Thread):** USB 3.0 signaling radiates broadband 2.4–2.5 GHz noise — measured **~20 dB noise-floor rise** from one external USB3 drive; extension cables restore performance; a dongle stacked on a USB3 port suffered "complete signal loss" [VERIFIED-current: Intel whitepaper 327216-001, https://www.usb.org/sites/default/files/327216.pdf, fetched 2026-07-19]; vendor echo for this exact device class: without the extension cable the stick "may not work at all… pairing issues, device dropouts, unreachable devices" [VERIFIED-current: https://www.home-assistant.io/connectzbt1/, fetched 2026-07-19]. Practice: both radios on USB-2 ports, each on its own ≥0.5 m extension, antennas on opposite desk sides [inference — application of the two sources].
- **Can a same-desk Thread radio degrade the ch20 fleet? Yes, plausibly — plan for it:** Silabs AN1017 isolation math: a −85 dBm 802.15.4 packet survives only if average interferer energy ≤ −22 dBm at the radio input; a +10 dBm nearby transmitter therefore needs "**32 dB or more antenna isolation**"; "far-away channel" operation performs "up to 20 dB" better than adjacent-channel [VERIFIED-current: AN1017, hy-line mirror, fetched 2026-07-19]. A Thread RCP centimeters from the coordinator antenna will not have 32 dB of path loss → **ch 25/26 + ~1 m antenna separation + separate extensions is the mitigation floor**; an empirical same-desk 15.4-vs-15.4 measurement does not exist anywhere we could find (§8) and is a natural first Matter-bench scenario [inference — AN1017 applied; measurement gap named]. **STOP-boundary restated:** any coexistence step that would imply re-channeling, re-joining, or otherwise perturbing the live fleet (5 devices/5 entities, ch20/panId 0x774c) is out of scope by ruling — none is proposed here.

## §3 — Commissioning flows, enumerated

### 3.1 The onboarding payload, field-precise

**QR (`MT:` + Base38):** packed little-endian bit string → Base38 (3-byte→5-char groups) [VERIFIED-current: Matter spec ch. 5 mirror, https://cookie-daily.life/src/matter/matter_html/Chapter%205.%20Commissioning.html, fetched 2026-07-19]:

| Field | Bits | Controller's use |
|---|---|---|
| Version | 3 | currently 0b000 |
| Vendor ID / Product ID | 16+16 | UX display · DCL lookup · cross-check vs Device Attestation |
| Custom Flow | 2 | 0=standard · 1=user-intent (user must trigger pairing mode) · 2=custom/vendor-app |
| Discovery Capabilities | 8 | transport selection bitmask: `kSoftAP=1<<0`, `kBLE=1<<1`, `kOnNetwork=1<<2`, `kWiFiPAF=1<<3` (1.4.x), `kNFC=1<<4` (1.6) [VERIFIED-current: https://raw.githubusercontent.com/project-chip/connectedhomeip/master/src/setup_payload/SetupPayload.h, fetched 2026-07-19; matter.js added the NFC bit in v0.17.5, 2026-07-13 [VERIFIED-current: matter.js CHANGELOG]] |
| Discriminator | 12 | filter/match BLE adverts or DNS-SD `_L<12-bit>`/`_S<4-bit>` subtypes |
| Setup Passcode | 27 | SPAKE2+ input for PASE (never transmitted; trivial values forbidden) |
| Padding + TLV extensions | var | optional manufacturer/common elements |

**Manual pairing code:** 11-digit (no VID/PID, no discovery caps, no TLV; only the **4-bit short discriminator** survives; Verhoeff check digit) · 21-digit adds 5-digit VID + 5-digit PID (required for custom-flow devices) [VERIFIED-current: spec ch. 5 mirror + https://docs.espressif.com/projects/esp-matter/en/latest/esp32/faq.html, fetched 2026-07-19]. Consequence: manual-code commissioning must try all discovery methods and matches on the short discriminator only [inference — from the missing fields].

### 3.2 The BLE role by device class

- **Factory-new WiFi device: BLE is the only path that exists in shipped retail practice.** SoftAP is in the spec but "not fully supported in the upstream connectedhomeip since Matter v1.0… no chances of supporting this in subsequent releases" (Espressif, whose silicon dominates WiFi Matter) [VERIFIED-current: https://github.com/espressif/esp-matter/issues/467, fetched 2026-07-19] → retail WiFi Matter devices are effectively 100% BLE-commissioned [inference — SDK non-support + no counter-example found]. WiFi-PAF/USD (1.4.2, "in place of BLE") has no shipped device/controller support found [VERIFIED-current: https://www.hackster.io/news/connectivity-standards-alliance-drops-bluetooth-le-for-wi-fi-only-provisioning-in-matter-1-4-2-29bd8e8e21d1, fetched 2026-07-19; Linux-side support = gap §8].
- **No BLE needed:** Ethernet devices (advertise `_matterc._udp` immediately) · already-on-network devices · any second-fabric join through an open commissioning window (§3.5) [VERIFIED-current: spec ch.5 mirror + https://mattercoder.com/codelabs/how-to-use-multi-admin/, fetched 2026-07-19].
- **Factory-new Thread device:** BLE required today (NFC is the future alternative).

## §5 — Lab-network isolation for the Matter bench

### 5.1 What Matter-over-WiFi actually needs from the LAN

- **Service discovery:** `_matterc._udp` (commissionable) + `_matter._tcp` (operational — the `_tcp` label does not mean TCP is used) + `_matterd._udp` (commissioner discovery, UDC targets) over standard mDNS: UDP 5353 to `224.0.0.251` (IPv4) and `ff02::fb` (IPv6) [VERIFIED-current: https://developers.home.google.com/matter/primer/commissionable-and-operational-discovery + https://project-chip.github.io/connectedhomeip-doc/tips_and_troubleshooting/discovery_from_a_host_computer.html + https://riddix.github.io/home-assistant-matter-hub/guides/connectivity-issues, fetched 2026-07-19]. A device answering only via IPv6 multicast is conformant → **the bench must pass `ff02::fb` at minimum; an IPv4-multicast-only mDNS path is insufficient** [inference — IPv6-first design + community report that IPv4 mDNS "advertises services but isn't required"].
- **IPv6, the real shape:** Matter *requires* IPv6 between controller and device; IPv4 is optional-extra per stack ("Matter requires IPv6, but optionally also allows IPv4") [VERIFIED-current: https://raw.githubusercontent.com/project-chip/matter.js/main/docs/TROUBLESHOOTING.md, fetched 2026-07-19]; "any IPv6-bearing network is suitable," no IPv6 internet or DHCPv6 needed [VERIFIED-current: https://handbook.buildwithmatter.com/how-it-works/connectivity-transports/ + https://www.home-assistant.io/integrations/matter/, fetched 2026-07-19]. **Link-local (fe80::/64) is sufficient when controller and device share one L2 segment** ("it will just work out of the box… pfSense has no role in intra-VLAN communication") [community-reported: https://forum.netgate.com/topic/198046/ + a UniFi user with ISP-IPv6 fully disabled]; **routable ULA/GUA becomes necessary the moment you leave one link** — cross-VLAN designs ("link-local alone will not work across VLANs; configure ULA" [VERIFIED-current: riddix guide]) and always for Thread-via-TBR (the OMR/RIO machinery — the banked failure domain §2.1). **Commissioning is the most same-segment-sensitive phase** — it hands around fe80:: addresses whose interface scope must be right (live failure exhibit: server guessed the wrong interface) [VERIFIED-current: https://github.com/home-assistant/android/issues/4528, fetched 2026-07-19].
- **Ports/transports:** UDP 5540 (PASE/CASE + operational; MRP reliability); TCP 5540 alongside for Matter 1.5+ large-payload ("full support for operation over TCP… large data sets" — port assumption flagged §8-13) [VERIFIED-current: https://csa-iot.org/newsroom/matter-1-5-introduces-cameras-closures-and-enhanced-energy-management-capabilities/, fetched 2026-07-19]; Matter 1.5 cameras are WebRTC (STUN/TURN/ICE dynamic UDP — trivial on a shared segment, hard across a restrictive firewall) [VERIFIED-current: same CSA page]. Never architect around IPv4-only; treat IPv4 operation as a per-device accident [inference — §5.1 evidence].

### 5.2 The options (2–3 concrete, costed, tradeoffs — NO winner; the ruling is design-phase work)

**Option A — bench SSID on the existing AP, same L2/subnet (client isolation OFF).**
What it buys: credential separation only. Same broadcast/multicast domain (bench mDNS chatter reaches every house host), no L3 separation, no airtime isolation (same radio; low-rate multicast + a misbehaving client degrade the whole BSS [inference — 802.11 shared medium + RFC 9119 low-rate-multicast cost, https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/rfc9119/]). Why it stays on the table: it is exactly the flat-network posture HA documents as the reliable one ("keep your network topology as simple and flat as possible") [VERIFIED-current: HA Matter docs]; link-local + mDNS + commissioning + WebRTC all just work. **Cost: $0.** Fine print: guest-SSID client isolation silently breaks controller↔device unicast — must be OFF [community-reported: https://community.tp-link.com/us/home/forum/topic/654996].

**Option B — bench VLAN + routed subnet + mDNS reflection.**
True L3 isolation + per-flow policy + a pcap choke point; broadcast domains separated. The plumbing it demands: UDP/TCP 5540 + UDP 5353 rules; **routable IPv6 (ULA minimum) on both VLANs with per-VLAN RA strictly confined** (leaked RAs silently rewrite victim-segment routing — the §2.1 failure domain generalized); and an mDNS reflector that actually handles Matter — which is the broken link: **mdns-repeater and everything built on it (HA multicast add-on, OPNsense plugin) is IPv4-only — it cannot reflect Matter's primary discovery transport** [community-reported: https://github.com/geekman/mdns-repeater/issues/3 + https://github.com/home-assistant/plugin-multicast/issues/21 + https://github.com/opnsense/plugins/issues/3352]; avahi's reflector does IPv6 but reflects records whose AAAA is an unreachable link-local ("partially works but requires sophisticated filtering" [community-reported: https://community.home-assistant.io/t/750324]); the widely-cited community reference flatly says "do not even attempt cross-VLAN mDNS/Matter" [community-reported: https://www.derekseaman.com/2023/10/part-3-smart-home-matter-and-thread-deep-dive.html]. **It also re-exposes the conntrack hazard:** all operational traffic now crosses a stateful firewall; long-idle UDP 5540 subscription flows get state-evicted — the banked battery-device "drop subscriptions every 15–30 min" class [banked: A1 return §6.5, matterjs-server docs verbatim]; mitigations exist (state-timeout tuning, sloppy-state rules [community-reported: https://ndo.dev/blog/hassio-thread-setup/ + opnsense #5543]; OpenWrt hardware flow-offload has a confirmed long-lived-UDP-killing bug — disable it [community-reported: https://github.com/openwrt/openwrt/issues/17915]). **Hardware/cost:** VLAN router you control (existing, else ~$60–150) + smart switch (~$30) + VLAN-capable AP (used enterprise ~$25–60) ≈ **~$90–260 if from scratch** [inference — street-price ballparks, unverified individually].

**Option C — dedicated bench AP + dual-homed controller (no reflection anywhere).**
A physically separate bench WiFi (cheap AP / OpenWrt travel router / hostapd on the controller); the controller sits **ON** the bench segment (second NIC or by being the AP) and reaches the house via its other interface. Nothing crosses segments because nothing needs to: discovery, commissioning, subscriptions, WebRTC all stay on-link; **link-local IPv6 suffices; zero RAs; zero reflectors; zero conntrack in the Matter path**. Community validation: a k8s build gave matter-server a dedicated interface on an isolated VLAN (`--primary-interface net1`) — full isolation, no reflector, working [community-reported: https://blog.davidmonro.net/index.php/2025/09/30/home-assistant-matter-over-wifi-and-isolated-vlans/]. Controller care: bind mDNS/operational traffic to the bench interface (matter-server `--primary-interface`; multi-interface ambiguity is a known commissioning failure [VERIFIED-current: riddix guide + HA android #4528]); disable IPv6 forwarding on the controller so it never becomes an accidental bench↔house router [VERIFIED-current: matter.js TROUBLESHOOTING.md]. Hazards specific to C: hostapd on a Pi's onboard Broadcom radio is the weak variant (firmware station-count limits; open brcmfmac crash reports — fine for 2–5 DUTs, not a 15-device soak [community-reported: https://github.com/raspberrypi/linux/issues/3010 + /7033]); the bench AP is a new 2.4 GHz interferer — channel-plan it against the house WiFi AND the ch20 fleet: **house WiFi ch 1 + bench WiFi ch 6 (or 11)** keeps Zigbee ch20 in a gap; never 40 MHz width on 2.4; a 5 GHz-capable bench SSID sidesteps it entirely for dual-band DUTs [VERIFIED-current: https://zigbeeguru.com/zigbee-channels-eu/ pairing table + https://www.oscium.com/training/zigbee-wifi-coexistence; inference — application to this floor plan]. **Cost:** GL.iNet GL-MT3000 ≈ $90 [VERIFIED-current: https://www.gl-inet.com/en-us/products/gl-mt3000] · GL.iNet Opal-class ≈ $35–40 · used dumb AP ≈ $20–40 · hostapd-on-Pi $0; + USB NIC ~$10–20 if needed [inference — ballparks]. **≈ $35–110 typical.**

### 5.3 The hazard ledger (applies regardless of option)

1. **mDNS across VLANs:** reflected records carry unreachable link-local AAAAs (discovery "succeeds," connection fails); the standard reflectors don't speak IPv6; dual reflection paths loop; reflected caches go stale [community-reported: §5.2-B citations; loop/TTL mechanics inference from mDNS design].
2. **IPv6 RA leakage between segments:** a mis-tagged trunk, a bridged test AP, or an OTBR advertising RIOs on the wrong interface silently rewrites routing on the victim segment — the §2.1 TBR failure domain generalized; the all-on-one-link design needs zero RAs (a real robustness argument for A/C) [community-reported: ndo.dev + netgate 198046; inference — generalization directed by the banked record].
3. **Stateful-firewall subscription death:** the banked 15–30-min class [banked: A1 return §6.5]; same-segment designs are immune by construction; the exact interval deserves a bench measurement (§7 N-4).
4. **Client isolation silently breaking unicast:** devices look online (DHCP/internet fine) but are uncontrollable [community-reported: tp-link 654996]. Check the switch too (port isolation/private VLAN).
5. **WiFi power-save vs multicast:** 802.11 multicast is unACKed, lowest-rate, DTIM-buffered when any station sleeps (loss "frequently exceeding 5%") — battery/ICD DUTs can miss mDNS; AP "multicast optimization"/IGMP-snooping features are named failure causes by HA and matter-hub docs (problem gear named: Archer AX50, UniFi APs) [VERIFIED-current: https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/rfc9119/ + HA Matter docs + riddix guide]. Bench AP: DTIM 1–2, multicast-enhancement OFF [inference — settings from the mechanism].
6. **Commissioning > steady-state strictness:** BLE (controller-side) + `_matterc._udp` + PASE to a typically-link-local address → the commissioning controller effectively must sit on the device's L2 (or deliver credentials over BLE and finish on-segment) [VERIFIED-current: HA android #4528; community-reported: davidmonro.net].

### 5.4 What the community record and our Zigbee posture teach

Across ~10 threads/blogs the pattern is uniform: **same-subnet is the reliable posture for Matter; cross-VLAN is achievable only at expert-tier effort, and most who try retreat** ("Matter won't work across vlan — HA must share network access with Matter devices" [community-reported: https://community.home-assistant.io/t/894826]; "problem completely disappeared when the dimmer was moved" [community-reported: t/750324]; NAT66 hacks as the workaround of last resort [community-reported: t/950269]). HA's own docs institutionalize it [VERIFIED-current: HA Matter docs]. The Zigbee bench's own isolation posture teaches the same lesson in radio form: isolation by *dedicated channel/PAN + physical discipline*, not by mid-path translation — the working analogue of Option C's "controller on the bench segment" [inference — posture analogy; the Zigbee bench's ch20/0x774c separation is containment at the edge, not reflection through a middle]. **No winner is declared: A is the $0 control condition, B is documented-flaky-but-truly-firewalled, C is the isolated shape the community record validates. Concrete-enough-to-cost: A $0 · B ~$90–260 · C ~$35–110.**

## §6 — Commissioning custody (what a Matter controller persists — mapped, not designed)

House precedent: the SD-5 seed-custody discipline [banked: pm-handoff v33 beats 2/4]. This section maps what EXISTS per stack; the design phase writes the custody section.

### 6.1 The credential taxonomy (from the SDK's own key allocator — stack-independent namespace)

[VERIFIED-current: https://raw.githubusercontent.com/project-chip/connectedhomeip/master/src/lib/support/DefaultStorageKeyAllocator.h, fetched 2026-07-19; fabric composition cross-checked at https://handbook.buildwithmatter.com/how-it-works/fabric/]

| Item | What it is | If LOST | If STOLEN (sensitivity) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Fabric root CA private key** | signs every NOC in the fabric (the controller IS the CA) | cannot mint/renew NOCs → cannot commission or re-cert | **CRITICAL** — forge a NOC for any node ID; impersonate admin; join covertly |
| RCAC (root CA cert) | fabric trust anchor (public) | trust anchor gone → fabric unverifiable | LOW alone (public) |
| Controller NOC + **operational private key** | the controller's own on-fabric identity; CASE authentication | controller loses its identity | **CRITICAL** — authenticate as the controller to every device |
| **IPK** (Identity Protection Key) | fabric-wide symmetric key; required input to the CASE Sigma1 Destination Identifier (`GenerateCaseDestinationId(ipk, …)`) [VERIFIED-current: …/src/protocols/secure_channel/CASEDestinationId.h] | cannot initiate CASE / discover own fabric's nodes | HIGH — de-anonymize fabric traffic, spoof destination IDs. GroupKeySetID 0 is reserved to it and non-removable [community-reported: connectedhomeip #28518]. **chip-tool's default IPK is the hardcoded ASCII `"temporary ipk 01"`** — a non-secret test IPK [VERIFIED-current: …/src/lib/support/TestGroupData.h] |
| Fabric/node IDs, fabric index, metadata | identity scoping numbers | — | LOW (identifiers) |
| Issued device NOCs | **NOT retained** — the controller keeps the CA key, not copies of leaf certs; devices hold their own [inference — no issued-NOC key exists in the allocator; custody asymmetry stated] | n/a | n/a |
| ACL state | authorization entries | authz rules lost | HIGH — self-granted privilege |
| CASE session resumption state | resumption ID → **shared secret** + peer CATs | full CASE re-handshake (slower; no security loss) | HIGH — contains shared secrets |
| Group keys/keysets + counters | symmetric group-cast keys | group multicast breaks | MEDIUM-HIGH — decrypt/inject group traffic |
| PAA attestation trust store | DCL-derived roots vetting device DACs at commissioning | can't verify new devices | see §6.4 — poisoned=integrity, stale=availability |
| Operational discovery cache | last-known per-node address/discovery data | re-resolve via mDNS (slower) | LOW-MEDIUM (inventory privacy) |

### 6.2 Per-stack storage (the A1 option set walked)

- **matterjs-server (A1 card a)** [banked: A1 return §6.2 — verified there 2026-07-19]: `~/.matter_server` (`/data` in containers) — one-file-per-key `server` dir + `certificates` (downloaded chains) + `vendors`; legacy `chip.json` "contains the global configuration of your Matter fabric, including IDs, keys, and certificates"; `--fabricid`/`--vendorid` process-start-only; storage-dir loss ⇒ re-commission the fleet.
- **chip-tool:** default **`/tmp/chip_tool_config.ini`** ("`$TMPDIR`, with fallback to `/tmp`"; per-identity `.ini` files) — the `.ini` IS the KVS: Base64 values keyed by the §6.1 namespace, **including the fabric root CA private key** (`ExampleOpCredsCAKey` — chip-tool is its own ad-hoc CA via `ExampleOperationalCredentialsIssuer`), **unencrypted at rest, in `/tmp`, i.e. fabric lost on reboot by default**; relocate with `--storage-directory <dir>` on *every* command [VERIFIED-current: examples/chip-tool/README.md + CHIPCommand.h + ExampleOperationalCredentialsIssuer.cpp, fetched 2026-07-19]. (`/tmp/chip_kvs` is the Linux *device/server* KVS path, distinct [VERIFIED-current: src/platform/Linux/CHIPPlatformConfig.h].)
- **CHIP Java bindings (A1 cards b/c):** everything persists through `PersistentStorageDelegate`; **two build modes**: the desktop `java-matter-controller` example compiles with `JAVA_MATTER_CONTROLLER_TEST` → uses the SAME `ExamplePersistentStorage` `.ini` + `ExampleOpCredsCAKey` ad-hoc CA as chip-tool (persistent, not ephemeral-per-run); the Android path uses `SharedPreferences` (`chip.platform.KeyValueStore`, app-private) + `AndroidOperationalCredentialsIssuer` (`AndroidDeviceControllerKey`) [VERIFIED-current: src/controller/java/AndroidDeviceControllerWrapper.cpp + PreferencesKeyValueStoreManager.java, fetched 2026-07-19]. In-tree TODOs acknowledge production IPK init is incomplete (test `DefaultIpkValue`) [VERIFIED-current: same files].
- **matter.js library (the substrate under card a):** default **`$HOME/.matter`** (`.matter-<env>` for named environments); file driver = one-value-per-file; sqlite option on Node ≥22. A ControllerNode persists contexts: **`certificates`** (`rootKeyPair` — the CA private key — + certs; "a new certificate is only created when the storage does not contain any credentials"), **`fabrics`** (fabric config incl. operational `keyPair` + `identityProtectionKey`), **`sessions`** (CASE resumption: `sharedSecret` + resumptionId), **`nodes`** (`commissionedNodes` address/discovery cache) [VERIFIED-current: packages/nodejs/src/environment/NodeJsEnvironment.ts + protocol/src/certificate/CertificateAuthority.ts + fabric/Fabric.ts + session/SessionManager.ts + MatterController.ts, fetched 2026-07-19].
- **Pure Java (card d):** nothing exists to map; the taxonomy in §6.1 is the from-scratch persistence obligation [inference — definitional].

### 6.3 Backup/restore reality

- **Cold-copy of the storage directory is a valid backup** — documented for matter.js (nodejs-shell migration: "Stop the shell," copy the files) [VERIFIED-current: packages/nodejs-shell/README.md, fetched 2026-07-19]; holds for chip-tool by construction (single `.ini`) [inference — single-file design; no explicit doc sentence].
- **Nothing is portable between stacks:** no export/migration tool exists anywhere; formats diverge (inipp `.ini` Base64 vs matter.js per-file contexts vs SharedPreferences) — moving stacks = hand-written converter or re-commission the fleet [inference — verified absence + format divergence; maintainer validation recommended §8-16].
- **The HA add-on story (the production precedent):** full backups include add-on `/data` → restore *should* preserve the fabric; community reality is noisier (post-restore devices offline, traced to network re-resolution, no clean zero-touch confirmation) — treat "restore preserves the fabric" as **plausible-but-fragile, not doc-guaranteed** [VERIFIED-current: https://www.home-assistant.io/common-tasks/general/; community-reported: https://community.home-assistant.io/t/matter-configuration-after-restore/820604] [inference — adjudication].
- **Design-phase consequence (named, not designed):** fabric identity = a SecretStore-class custody object (INV-SE-03 interaction); the backup unit is the whole storage tree, cold; loss ⇒ fleet re-commissioning; theft ⇒ full-admin capability — feeds A1 §10 Q2 [banked: A1 return §10-2] [inference].

### 6.4 The attestation trust store as custody

- **chip-tool:** `--paa-trust-store-path <dir>`; **without it, the built-in TEST PAA store is used** (a stock chip-tool trusts test-signed devices); `--bypass-attestation-verifier` exists [VERIFIED-current: docs/development_controllers/chip-tool/chip_tool_guide.md, fetched 2026-07-19].
- **matter.js/matterjs-server:** `DclCertificateService` — optional bundled cert seed, then live DCL updates on a **daily** default timer; CRL fetched lazily; attestation enforced in `ControllerCommissioningFlow` with an `onFailure` policy (reject / accept-with-logging / callback) [VERIFIED-current: packages/protocol/src/dcl/DclCertificateService.ts + peer/ControllerCommissioningFlow.ts, fetched 2026-07-19] — the A1 [BLOCKING] DCL-outbound-vs-INV-LF question rides on exactly this surface [banked: A1 return §10-1].
- **Failure classes:** poisoned store (rogue PAA added) = **integrity** failure — counterfeit devices commission clean; stale/unreachable = **availability** failure — legitimately-attested new devices rejected at commissioning; steady-state operation unaffected (CASE uses NOCs, not PAAs; the DCL gate is commissioning-time only) [inference — grounded in the attestation-at-commissioning flow].

### 6.5 Published security analyses (one-liners for the design phase's threat frame)

- KU Leuven COSIC, ePrint 2025/1268: ProVerif analysis — "multiple cryptographic design flaws, including low-entropy passcodes, static salts, and weak PBKDF2 parameters," security decisions delegated to vendors [VERIFIED-current: https://eprint.iacr.org/2025/1268.pdf, fetched 2026-07-19].
- USF/NIST 2023: Matter's trust is asymmetric — devices attested, "the commissioner is not vetted"; authors commissioned a commercial device with an unauthorized controller [VERIFIED-current: https://tsapps.nist.gov/publication/get_pdf.cfm?pub_id=956487, fetched 2026-07-19].
- NDSS 2025 ("Hidden and Lost Control"): **hidden fabric credentials as covert control channels**; forged controller identities evading removal; temporary-access attackers retaining persistent control [VERIFIED-current: https://www.ndss-symposium.org/wp-content/uploads/2025-266-paper.pdf, fetched 2026-07-19].
- Black Hat EU 2024 (Bitdefender): CVE-2024-3297 (Sigma1 replay → CASE DoS), CVE-2024-3454 — session/discovery, not credential theft [VERIFIED-current: https://i.blackhat.com/EU-24/Presentations/EU-24-Genge-BreakingMatterVulnerabiltiesInTheMatterProtocol-wp.pdf, fetched 2026-07-19].
