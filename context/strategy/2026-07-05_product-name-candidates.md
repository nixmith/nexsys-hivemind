# Consumer Product-Name Candidates (under the Asimtote parent)

**Date:** 2026-07-05
**Goal:** a warm, pronounceable, *spellable* product brand (the thing a homeowner buys) with a **free exact `.com`**, sitting under **Asimtote** (parent/engine) and extending by edition (Pro / Enterprise / Hub).
**Method:** ~48 candidates generated across hearth / home / calm / core / light / steady themes; each exact `.com` checked live via Google DNS (NXDOMAIN = unregistered). Availability is a strong signal, not a registrar guarantee — confirm before purchase, and run formal trademark clearance on your shortlist.

---

## The one big finding

**Pretty → taken. Coined → free.** Almost every real-word compound (emberly, hearthly, dwellwell, corehaus, serenova, nestara…) is already registered or parked-for-sale — domainers and founders grab those first. The names with a *free* exact `.com` are all invented. The most productive on-brand vein is the **"‑kor" suffix = "core"** — it encodes your actual concept (the core of the home), and it's reliably available.

This is the tradeoff behind your whole exercise: a meaningful, easy real word almost never comes with a free `.com`; a distinctive coinage does. These candidates optimize for *coined-but-warm*.

## How it fits with Asimtote

```
Asimtote            parent company + the technical engine ("Asimtote Core")
   └── Hearthkor    the consumer product a homeowner buys
          ├── Hearthkor            (the home app/appliance)
          ├── Hearthkor Pro        prosumer tier
          ├── Hearthkor Enterprise B2B / multi-site
          └── Hearthkor Hub        hardware, later
```

- **Asimtote** stays the durable, austere parent (and the developer/engine brand — technical folks are fine with the coined name and it carries the asymptote-honesty metaphor).
- **The product brand** is warm, sayable, spellable — the thing strangers touch — and extends by descriptor, so you never mint a second coined name per segment (the UniFi model).
- The "‑kor" ending is a deliberate bridge: warm stem (hearth/haven/ember) + a solid "core" — *infrastructure-grade, presented with consumer calm*, which is exactly your brand's north star. If you want something purely soft/consumer, `koridel`/`welkora` lean that way at the cost of a clear meaning.

## My recommendation

**Hearthkor** or **Havenkor**, with **Emberkor** a close third.** All three are warm, meaningful, on-brand, collision-free, and `.com`-available. Between them:

- **Hearthkor** — most evocative (the hearth *is* the home's core; ties to warmth + gathering).
- **Havenkor** — most trust-forward (safety/refuge), closest to the "calm, reliable, protects your home" promise.
- **Emberkor** — best metaphor for *reliability* (the coal that never goes out), slightly more distinctive sound.
