# TAMORO — What the Mark Will Actually Brand (Product One-Pager for Counsel)

*Companion to Part 8 of the July 12, 2026 memorandum. Purpose: let you refine the draft identifications against the real product and roadmap, and judge bona fide intent item-by-item.*

## The product in one paragraph

A **consumer smart-home operating system**: locally-run hub software that connects, monitors, automates, and secures the devices in a home (sensors, switches, plugs, lights, locks, cameras), with a companion web dashboard. Its differentiator is **honesty/verifiability**: the system never reports a device action as confirmed unless it actually confirmed it, keeps a complete replayable event log, and can show the user *why* any automation fired, why it didn't, and whether it truly happened. All core function is local-first (works without the cloud); cloud services are a roadmap layer, not a dependency. AI capability (occupant-preference learning, ambient automation) is deliberately incremental, advisory-before-autonomous, and **field-limited to residential use** — which is both the product philosophy and, per the memo, the identification-drafting strategy.

## Development stage (bona fide intent evidence)

Working software exists and is substantially developed: the hub engine passed an internal certification in July 2026 (multi-day continuous soak on real radio hardware and devices; ~1,700+ command verdicts recorded with zero false confirmations), runs on commodity hub hardware (Raspberry-Pi-class), speaks the Zigbee device protocol via a USB radio, and has a working observability dashboard. Second-wave device integrations are in hand and being commissioned now. Pre-launch, pre-revenue; no public use of any mark yet. Development records (design documents, build history, bench/certification records) are dated and retained — available if useful for intent documentation.
