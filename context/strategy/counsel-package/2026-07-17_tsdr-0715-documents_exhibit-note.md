## Consequences for the program (screens/inference; counsel to confirm)

1. **§1c ("TOMORO won't be defended") is WEAKENED, not broken.** The "unanswered Final OA / two firms who could trivially fix it and haven't" pillar is gone — they fixed it, on the last possible day, by the cheapest possible mechanism. The use-based decay clocks are untouched (UK/EU non-use revocation ~2029-03; US §71 starvation ~2031-32 — no US use dates exist, Exhibit H) — the long-game asymmetry stands. But the "nobody is minding the file" assumption is dead: someone is minding it, minimally.
2. **A narrowed-but-broad US TOMORO registration is now the expected outcome** (publication → opposition window → registration). The memo's §6.2 "moderate" §2(d)-citation tier is the live branch, with one adverse sharpening: the surviving Class 9 contains field-unlimited AI-software items, so the goods-gap argument must lean on overall commercial impression (enterprise/generative-AI agents) rather than an explicit enterprise limitation.
